The skills

The story

I’ve been doing “programming” since I was in my teens, but I’ll spare you those boring stories.

During college, I majored in business, took a bunch of computer science classes, and hung around the computer lab a bit too much. During one summer, I interned in the Information Systems group for Nycomed Pharmaceuticals. This was my first practical exposure to business systems and I remain grateful for the opportunity.

After graduating from the College of William and Mary, I spent 8 years working at American Management Systems (which became CACI following the dissolution of the former) in a variety of capacities - acting as everything from a technical writer to analyst to build/configuration manager to lead developer. Eventually I became something like a combination project manager and technical lead. There we re-built Powerbuilder applications and other legacy systems using J2EE. Application servers like WebLogic and JBoss (huzzah!!) crawled out of the evolutionary mud, crashed frequently, and sometimes we cried and/or drank beer. We experienced this first hand; caches were built and refined; we read “Design Patterns” and refactored; a good time was had by most.

In 2006 I joined Phase2 Technology. Similar to AMS, I’ve done many things along the way - testing, business analysis, client management, and development - and hopefully I’ve done them well. Along the way I’ve continued to learn technically - can I ask for anything more?
