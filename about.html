The skills

  • Java - I started developing in Java sometime around 2000; I spent a lot of my twenties developing enterprise Java applications using Swing and server-side EJB (with a bit of web stuff thrown in too - back when web sites were what you did when you grew tired of beating your head against tougher problems.) I considered myself a "state of the art" Java developer circa 2005 - using things like Ant, JUnit, JSTL, JAXP, and all the other 'acronyms' frequently. At one point, I was a Java Certified Programmer and for fun I grabbed a few other Java certifications as well.
  • Ruby - I’ve been developing in Ruby since 2011, and it’s currently my “go-to” language for pretty much, well, anything. I enjoy being astonished at its economy and grace. I'm still learning, but consider myself of intermediate skill in Ruby.
  • Rails - I’m not a Rails “hotshot” (yet) but I’ve developed a few applications in Rails, enjoy working with it as a framework, and continue to focus on growing in this area. Given my (occasionally painful) time spent developing enterprise applications using J2EE, Rails is a breath of fresh air. That said, I try not to let the ease of Rails make me forget to understand what the hell is actually happening 'under the covers'.
  • HTML & CSS - I can write CSS and troubleshoot others’ CSS and HTML. I can troubleshoot positioning, manage floats, implement sprites, and know why you do a CSS reset. I’m capable of implementing intermediate-level HTML and CSS layouts from PSDs. I have experience with both Bootstrap and Foundation (in case that matters to you) and have started to utilize Sass over the last year. I can do a bit of JQuery. I'm not an expert front-end developer though, and wonder if I'll ever be!
  • Other -

    • I know Git and I'm comfortable with advanced configuration management workflows. In a previous job, I championed a fairly sophisticated branching and release management scheme before it was cool to do so.
    • I have considerable experience with sophisticated database schemas, and I’m able to develop complex queries, analyze problematic SQL, and optimize.
    • I know and have practiced Agile, and can troubleshoot process issues and lead a technical team. I’m a "certified" Scrummaster as well as a proudly expired PMP.
    • I'm an Atlassian fanboy - I'm adept at configuration and administration of JIRA and Confluence.
    • Finally, I have a good deal of expertise in Drupal and the overall processes, strategies, and tactics involved in building high-traffic, high-profile websites - but I am not a Drupal or PHP developer.

The story

I’ve been doing “programming” since I was in my teens, but I’ll spare you those boring stories.

In college, I majored in business, took a bunch of computer science classes, and hung around the computer lab a bit too much. One summer, I interned in the Information Systems group for Nycomed Pharmaceuticals. This was my first practical exposure to business systems and I remain grateful for the opportunity.

After graduating from the College of William and Mary, I spent 8 years working at American Management Systems (which became CACI following the dissolution of the former) in a variety of capacities - acting as everything from a technical writer to analyst to build/configuration manager to lead developer. Eventually I became something like a combination project manager and technical lead. There we re-built Powerbuilder applications and other legacy systems using J2EE. Application servers like WebLogic and JBoss (hell yeah!!) crawled out of the evolutionary muck, crashed frequently, and we cried and/or drank beer. We experienced this first hand; caches were built and refined; we read “Design Patterns” and refactored; a good time was had by most.

In 2006 I joined Phase2 Technology. Similar to AMS, I’ve done many things along the way - testing, business analysis, consulting, client services, and development - and hopefully I’ve done them well.
